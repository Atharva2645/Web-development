About Lord Krishna

The Eighth Avatar of Lord Vishnu

Lord Krishna, born in Mathura to Devaki and Vasudeva, is one of the most revered and worshipped deities in Hinduism. He is considered the eighth avatar of Lord Vishnu and played a central role in the Mahabharata.

Krishna's childhood was spent in Vrindavan, where he performed numerous miracles and divine acts (leelas) with his devotees, especially the gopis (cowherd maidens) and his beloved Radha.

As a young prince, Krishna established the city of Dwarka and became the charioteer and guide to Arjuna in the Kurukshetra war, delivering the timeless wisdom of the Bhagavad Gita.
